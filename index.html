The Opportunity

One-person companies
are the fastest-growing
segment in tech.

AI has made it possible for a single person to build, ship, and scale a product. But building alone doesn't mean building in isolation. What's missing isn't tools — it's community, guidance, and a structured path forward.

01
Builders need direction
After shipping their first app, most solopreneurs don't know what comes next — marketing, monetisation, scaling.
02
Knowledge is fragmented
Expert insights are scattered across podcasts, threads, and paywalls. Builders need it in one room, from real practitioners.
03
Community is the moat
The most valuable networks aren't built on LinkedIn. They're built in rooms where people share real work and real feedback.

The Concept

A monthly series that
mirrors the founder journey.

Each episode of "What's Next" is designed around a different phase of building a one-person company — from first build to scaling revenue to reimagining what work means in an AI-native world.

"Not a meetup. Not a conference. A curated space where builders learn, demo, and get direct feedback — every month, at every stage."
